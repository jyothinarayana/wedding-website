How we met

We were in high school when our eyes first met. It wasn't love at first sight for us but the realization of "Oh! we are in the same boat", at first sight as we would often be late for the same classes and would find ourselves standing outside, waiting for the teacher to let us in.

Fast forward a decade, and we met again. But this time the background wasn't a classroom, it was the luscious greenery and the snow capped mountains of Switzerland. We couldn't help but fall in love. And once we discovered our mutual excitement for the adventurous outdoors, the deal was sealed. Do watch our video for a glimpse into our delightful memories.

20th May

Wedding 8 AM - 2 PM

This is the part when we will say our vows and 'I do' in traditional Indian Brahmin style. Some of the main events you will witness are Varapuje/Baraat (the bride's family welcome the groom and his family), Jaimala (the bride and groom exchange floral garlands), Kanyadaan (bride sits on her father's lap and he places her hands into the groom's hands as a gesture of bringing them together), Pradakshina/Phera (the bride and the groom go around the sacred fire), Mangalya (the groom ties the sacred yellow thread around the bride's neck), and Saptapadi (the bride and the groom take seven steps together on seven mounds of uncooked rice making promises on each step for a joyous and harmonious life ahead).
